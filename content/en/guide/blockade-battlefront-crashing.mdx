<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Stability Fixes">

If adjusting your in-game settings does not stop the crashes, the issue likely lies within your local system configurations or temporary files. Follow these steps to perform a complete optimization sweep.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Run Roblox as an Administrator">
    Right-click your **Roblox Player** shortcut on your desktop, select **Properties**, navigate to the **Compatibility** tab, check **Run this program as an administrator**, and click apply. This ensures Roblox has the permissions required to allocate system memory and write temporary shader data.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear Corrupted Roblox Cache">
    Press `Windows Key + R` to open the Run dialog box, type `%localappdata%\Roblox` and hit enter. Open the **Downloads** and **Versions** folders, then delete their contents to force Roblox to rebuild clean temporary assets upon your next launch.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Disable Performance-Heavy Overlays">
    Open Discord, go to **User Settings**, select **Game Overlay**, and turn off the in-game overlay. Do the same for NVIDIA GeForce Experience and Xbox Game Bar, as active overlay hooks frequently trigger instant desktop crashes during asset-heavy waves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Update Graphics Card Drivers">
    Open your NVIDIA App, AMD Adrenalin, or Intel Driver Utility to check for the latest stable release. Corrupted or outdated display drivers are a leading cause of screen freezes when complex particle effects render on screen.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Clean Installation">
If you continue to crash on startup, perform a clean reinstall of the Roblox Player. Uninstall the application via the Windows Control Panel, delete any leftover files in `%localappdata%\Roblox`, and download a fresh installer directly from the official Roblox website.
</Callout>

</Section>
