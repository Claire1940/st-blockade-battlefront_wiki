<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Acquiring Blockade Battlefront Skins: Rarity & Drop Rates">

Cosmetics play a massive role in defining your presence on the battlefield. In Blockade Battlefront, skins allow you to customize the appearance of various factions, including Cameramen, Speakermen, TV Men, and the opposing Toilet forces. These visual upgrades range from simple color swaps to complex model overhauls featuring custom animations and particle effects.

To obtain these cosmetics, players must navigate the in-game store and open crates using Points earned from matches or Elite Points acquired through special challenges. Understanding the drop rates is essential before investing your hard-earned currency into the gacha system.

|Rarity|Drop Rate|Average Cost|Visual Features|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Common**|50.0%|500 Points|Basic recolors, minor texture adjustments|
|**Uncommon**|30.0%|1,200 Points|Alternative clothing, basic headwear accessories|
|**Rare**|14.0%|3,000 Points|Distinct theme changes, minor model modifications|
|**Epic**|5.0%|7,500 Points|Unique model redesigns, basic glowing elements|
|**Legendary**|0.9%|20,000 Points|Full character overhauls, custom idle animations|
|**Mythic**|0.1%|Event Only|Unique particle trails, custom voice lines, screen effects|

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management">
Avoid spending all your Points on standard Common crates. Saving for Epic or event-specific crates yields a much higher return on investment if you want to stand out during matches.
</Callout>

Each crate has a specific pool of items. The standard Cosmetic Crate contains all permanent Common, Uncommon, and Rare skins. Elite Crates, which require Elite Points, guarantee at least an Epic drop and offer a small chance at Legendary cosmetics. Seasonal Event Crates are introduced during major updates and contain limited-time items that do not enter the standard pool once the event concludes.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Customization Slots and Loadout Setup">

The customization system allows players to mix and match different cosmetic components depending on the base unit. While full-body skins replace the entire model, accessory slots let you personalize individual parts of your character.

Understanding which slots are compatible with your favorite units prevents wasting points on incompatible accessories.

|Slot Type|Compatible Units|Customizable Elements|Restrictions|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Head**|All Units|Hats, screens, toilet lids, camera lenses|Cannot combine hats with full helmets|
|**Torso**|Cameramen, Speakermen|Coats, ties, armor plates, chest cores|Exclusive to humanoid factions|
|**Backpack**|Large Units|Jetpacks, speakers, battery packs|Requires unit to have a back attachment slot|
|**Aura**|All Units|Ambient particle trails, glow effects|Only available on Legendary and Mythic items|

<Callout type="info" title="Fair Play Standard">
All hitboxes remain identical regardless of the skin equipped. A larger skin like the Titan Golden Cameraman does not make your character easier to hit, maintaining competitive integrity.
</Callout>

To equip your unlocked items, head to the Customization tab from the main lobby. Select your desired unit class, choose the slot you wish to modify, and apply the unlocked skin. You can save up to three distinct cosmetic presets per unit class for quick swapping between rounds.
