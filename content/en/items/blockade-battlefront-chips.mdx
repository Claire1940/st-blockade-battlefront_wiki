blockade battlefront chips are optional upgrades that you buy from the shop, equip from your inventory, and then let work automatically once your character spawns. They do not behave like a passive currency sink or a one-time reward; they are a loadout choice that changes how durable or aggressive your selected character feels in a match.

<Callout type="tip" title="How the system behaves">
Once a chip is equipped, its bonus applies automatically on spawn. If you join a new server, equip it again before you expect value from it.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Rarity Breakdown and Stat Ranges">

The chip pool has six rarity tiers, and most of the value comes from the stat spread rather than a fixed power number. Regular chips roll random stats, while Secret chips use set values created through the admin panel.

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Rarity Note">
Regular chip stats are randomized, so two chips from the same rarity can still feel different. Do not judge a tier by tint alone.
</Callout>

| Rarity | Roll Chance | Visual Tint | Stat Pattern | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Common | 40% | Default | +10 to +500 HP | Most basic survivability roll |
| Common 1 | 40% | Pastel tint | +1% to +20% damage buff | Most basic damage roll |
| Rare | 15% | Blue | -1% to -20% damage income from all sources | Second-most common tier |
| Legendary | 5% | Orange | +10 to +500 HP, +1% to +20% damage buff, +1% to +20% damage reduction | Strong all-around chip |
| Mythic | 0.5% | Red, blacked out in shop | +500 to +1500 HP, +10% to +30% damage buff, -10% to -30% damage income from all sources | Rarest obtainable tier |
| Secret | Special | Green | Fixed stats set by a developer | Dev-only, tester-only, trusted players, or giveaway winners |

<Callout type="info" title="Stat Planning">
Because the rolls are randomized, a lower rarity chip with a strong roll can still be useful early. Legendary remains the cleanest long-term target for most players.
</Callout>

| Tier | Best Use Case | Practical Value |
|---|---|---|
| Common | Early durability | Easy to obtain, simple HP boost |
| Common 1 | Early offense | Cheap damage gain, flexible for newer builds |
| Rare | Situational utility | Useful when you want a different stat profile |
| Legendary | Balanced loadout | Best all-around tier for most players |
| Mythic | High-end chase | Excellent ceiling, extremely low roll chance |
| Secret | Admin access | Fixed specialty stats, not standard progression |

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Roll, Equip, and Re-equip">

The shop is the main route for most players, and the Value Pack stands out because it guarantees a Legendary chip plus 5 Astro Tokens. If you want steady progress, the order matters: roll smart, equip once you get a useful result, and remember the server re-equip rule.

<Callout type="tip" title="Best Workflow">
Treat chip hunting like a progression loop: get a roll, save the useful tier, equip it before combat, and re-equip after you switch servers.
</Callout>

  <Step num="1" title="Open the shop">

  <Step num="2" title="Pick your acquisition method">

  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Equip from inventory">

  <Step num="4" title="Spawn and test it">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Best Chip Choices by Playstyle">

There is no single universal best chip, because the right pick depends on whether you want toughness, damage, or a flexible middle ground. The safest rule is to match the chip to the job your character is doing in the match.

<Callout type="success" title="Build Logic">
Choose chips based on what your character lacks. Tanks want more durability, while aggressive builds want damage and a stronger payoff from each spawn.
</Callout>
