<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding the Dark Dimension Mechanics">

The **blockade battlefront dark dimension** represents the ultimate test of movement and awareness within the game. Unlike standard modes where offensive power determines success, this dimension prioritizes evasion, resource management, and environmental adaptation. Players are thrust into a near-pitch-black environment where traditional Titans and Special Titans are disabled, forcing a reliance on smaller, more agile units.

The most distinctive feature of this mode is the **Sanity Bar**. Displayed as a purple meter above your character's head, this bar constantly depletes. If the bar reaches zero, the primary antagonist of the mode, Jeffery, gains a significant speed boost, making him nearly impossible to outrun. To replenish this bar, you must navigate the darkness to find and collect randomly spawning purple orbs.

| Mechanic | Function | Impact on Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sanity Bar** | Tracks mental stability | Empty bar speeds up Jeffery significantly |
| **Purple Orbs** | Resource collection | Refills Sanity; only 7 can exist at once |
| **Jeffery** | Invincible stalker | Chases player throughout all four waves |
| **Darkness** | Visibility modifier | Limits sight, making orb spotting difficult |
| **Unit Lock** | Character restriction | Titans and Special Titans cannot be deployed |

<Callout type="tip" title="Orb Management">
Do not attempt to save orbs for later stages. Since the map caps the number of active orbs at seven, leaving them on the ground prevents new ones from spawning, potentially starving you of Sanity during the more chaotic final waves.
</Callout>

Survival in the dark dimension requires a shift in mindset. You are not the hunter; you are the prey. Every second spent standing still or focusing too heavily on combat increases the risk of being cornered by Jeffery or caught in a lethal trap.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Wave-by-Wave Walkthrough">

Navigating the **blockade battlefront dark dimension** requires precise knowledge of when new mechanics are introduced. The difficulty curve is steep, with each wave adding a layer of complexity that can end a run in seconds.

### Wave 1: The Initial Descent
The first wave introduces you to Jeffery. He is completely invulnerable to all damage types, so do not waste your abilities or time trying to fight him. Your primary goal is to maintain a constant distance while scanning the floor for purple orbs. While Jeffery is slow initially, the toilets that spawn in this wave are deceptively tanky.

### Wave 2: The Rising Spikes
In Wave 2, the environment itself becomes hostile. Spikes will begin to erupt from the ground at regular intervals. These spikes are particularly dangerous because they do not stop during "shop" moments or UI interactions. You must remain in constant motion to avoid being impaled.

| Wave | Primary New Threat | Strategy Adjustment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wave 1** | Jeffery & Latching Toilets | Focus on orb collection and kiting |
| **Wave 2** | Ground Spikes | Never stop moving, even during shop phases |
| **Wave 3** | Red Zones & The Eye | Watch the ground and stop moving for the Eye |
| **Wave 4** | Increased Speed | Maximum focus on movement and sanity |

### Wave 3: The Red Zone and The Eye
This is where most players fail. Wave 3 introduces the **Red Zone** mechanic, where large sections of the floor glow bright red. Standing in these zones deals approximately 1500 damage per tick, which can kill most characters almost instantly. Simultaneously, an "Eye" entity will appear. When the Eye opens, you must stop all movement immediately. If you move while the Eye is watching, you will take massive damage.

### Wave 4: The Final Sprint
Wave 4 does not introduce new mechanics but significantly increases the speed and frequency of all previous hazards. Spikes trigger faster, the Red Zones shift more rapidly, and Jeffery becomes more aggressive. Your Sanity bar will feel like it is draining faster simply because you have less time to safely path toward orbs.

<Callout type="warning" title="Hazard Overlap">
Be extremely careful when the Eye mechanic overlaps with a Red Zone. If the Eye opens while you are standing on red ground, you are forced to choose between taking 1500 damage from the ground or taking damage from the Eye. Always prioritize staying on neutral ground.
</Callout>

</Section>
