<Section icon={Target} title="Obtaining the SDFE0 Black Drive">

The first major bottleneck of the quest is securing the SDFE0 Black Drive (often referred to as the Dark Drive). This item does not drop from standard enemies or bosses. Instead, it is tied directly to the player-controlled TV race characters. 

To farm this item efficiently, you must understand the exact survival and drop mechanics implemented by the developers.

| Character | Allowed to Drop | Drop Chance | Key Rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| TV Man | Yes | 5% | Must survive 2 minutes |
| Scientist TV Man | Yes | 5% | Must survive 2 minutes |
| Large TV Man | Yes | 5% | Must survive 2 minutes |
| Titan TV Man | No | 0% | Excluded from drops |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Equip a TV Race Character">
    Select any standard TV race character such as TV Man, Scientist TV Man, or Large TV Man. Ensure you do not use Titan TV Man, as he is excluded from the drop pool.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enter a Private or Casual Server">
    Launch a private server or join a casual match. Private servers are highly recommended because they allow you to coordinate with friends without public interference.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Survive for Two Minutes">
    Once the match begins, you must stay alive for at least 2 minutes. If your character dies before the 2-minute mark, the drop rate is completely voided.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Coordinate the Death and Pickup">
    After 2 minutes have passed, let the toilets defeat your character. Because you cannot pick up your own dropped drive, a cooperative partner must grab it for you. Repeat the process so you can grab theirs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Co-op Is Essential">
Since self-pickup is disabled for your own dropped drive, the absolute fastest way to bypass this mechanic is to build a TV farm with a trusted friend in a private server. Alternate deaths every two minutes to guarantee both of you secure the drive.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="The 22 Missions of the Blockade Battlefront UTTVM Quest">

Once you hand the SDFE0 Black Drive to the Scientist TV Man, he will grant you access to his terminal, unlocking a massive list of 22 individual missions. These missions cover everything from character mastery to specific boss-slaying milestones. You must complete every single objective to progress to the final stage of the quest.

| Mission Objective | Best Game Mode | Average Time | Efficiency Rating |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Titan Request 30x | Boss Rush | 4 minutes | High |
| Special Titan Request 20x | Extreme Mode | 5 minutes | Medium |
| Scientist TV Mastery 80 | Zombie Mode | 15 minutes | High |
| Slay Infected Titans | Boss Rush | 8 minutes | High |

The most time-consuming part of this phase is reaching Mastery level 80 on your Scientist TV Man. To speed this up, utilize the Zombie Mode. Zombie Mode features high-density waves of infected enemies that provide massive chunks of mastery experience when cleared with area-of-effect abilities.

For the Titan Request missions, avoid public matchmaking servers. Public servers rarely progress past wave 70, making it incredibly difficult to trigger the necessary requests consistently. Instead, run Boss Rush solo or with a dedicated team. This mode allows you to cycle through boss phases rapidly, letting you complete your request quotas in a fraction of the time.

<Callout type="info" title="Mastery Grinding Tip">
When grinding mastery for the Scientist TV Man, equip any available 2x Mastery Boosts before starting a long Zombie Mode session. This cuts your total grinding time in half and helps you reach the level 80 requirement much faster.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Securing Flash Drives A through E">

Completing the 22 missions is only half the battle. The Scientist TV Man also requires you to recover five auxiliary flash drives: Drive A, B, C, D, and E. These drives are held by powerful late-game bosses and have a flat 10 percent drop rate. You will need to farm specific encounters repeatedly to collect the entire set.

| Drive | Source Boss | Drop Rate | Key Combat Tip |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Drive A | Astro Rocketeer | 10% | Dodge incoming rockets |
| Drive B | Song Clone | 10% | Maintain medium range |
| Drive C | Rocket Clone | 10% | Use high-damage units |
| Drive D | G-Clone | 10% | Focus fire on the core |
| Drive E | Trapper | 10% | Watch out for snare traps |

To successfully farm these bosses, your squad must bring a balanced mix of single-target damage and crowd control. The Trapper, in particular, can easily lock down your frontline units, leaving your base vulnerable to fast-moving toilets. Always prioritize disabling the support toilets before focusing your high-damage abilities on the main boss.

After turning in all five flash drives and completing the 22 missions, the final phase of the quest triggers. You must enter a special scenario where you play as Titan Cinema. During this encounter, you must allow yourself to be defeated by Jaman V2 and the infected Titan Speaker.

Utilize the heavy stationary guns and call in Camera or TV reinforcements to secure the victory. Once the Malware Toilet falls, you will receive the final unlock badge, officially adding the Upgraded Titan TV Man to your roster.
