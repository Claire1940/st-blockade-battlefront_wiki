<Section icon={Swords} title="Comprehensive Ability Breakdown">

To conquer the **blockade battlefront astro entrapper**, players must memorize its diverse move set. The boss utilizes three detaining claws that act as both offensive weapons and defensive shields. Unlike standard toilets, the Astro Entrapper is proactive, frequently dodging and counter-attacking.

| Ability Name | Effect Description | Damage / Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Passive: Dodge** | Automatically evades incoming projectiles and melee | N/A |
| **Catch** | Pulls in non-Titan units for an **instant kill** | Instant Death |
| **Orb Catch** | Catches energy orbs and reflects them to sender | Variable |
| **Laser Absorption** | Absorbs laser attacks to **heal health** | Healing |
| **Warp Attacks** | 6-8 rapid rams into the target | 200 per strike |
| **Claw Punch** | A heavy physical swing using claws | 500 damage |
| **Blade Attack** | Stabbing motion with two massive blades | 300 per hit |
| **Laser Fire** | Triple laser blast from detaining claws | 1800 - 3600 |

<Callout type="warning" title="Anti-Laser Warning">
Do **NOT** use units like the Camera Attack Helicopter or other laser-heavy Titans during the Laser Absorption phase. You will inadvertently heal the boss, potentially resetting minutes of progress.
</Callout>

The most dangerous mechanic for beginners is the **Catch** ability. If you are using smaller units like the Normal Cameraman or Engineer Cameraman, the Astro Entrapper will simply pull them in and eliminate them instantly. This makes the use of Titan-class characters or Large Speakermen mandatory for frontline tanking. Furthermore, the **Warp Attacks** can bypass your front line entirely, striking your backline support units if they aren't protected by AOE stuns or high-durability barriers.

### Advanced Combat Mechanics
The boss enters different phases as its health depletes. In Phase 3, the **Throw-Orb** ability becomes significantly more lethal. While it normally deals 600 damage to small characters, this spikes to 1200 damage in the final phase. For Titan characters, the damage increases from 300 to 600 per hit. This scaling means that a "slow and steady" approach becomes increasingly risky as the fight goes on.

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Boss Defeat Strategy">

Defeating the **blockade battlefront astro entrapper** requires a coordinated effort between damage dealers and support units. Follow these steps to ensure a successful raid.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Phase 1: Preparation and Positioning">
    Before Wave 80, clear out all minor toilets to ensure your Titans can focus exclusively on the Astro Entrapper. Place your high-HP Titans in the center of the path to bait the "Catch" ability, which they can resist.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Phase 2: Managing the Warp Attacks">
    When the boss begins its 6-8 warp sequence, do not panic. The strikes deal 200 damage each. Ensure your Engineer Cameramen are actively repairing your Titans or that you have Polycephaly providing screen-based disruption to slow the boss down.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Phase 3: Controlled Damage Output">
    Monitor the boss's claws. When they glow with a blue aura, it is preparing for **Laser Absorption**. Immediately cease all laser fire. Switch to physical attackers like Large Speakermen or Blade-wielding units to continue chip damage without healing the boss.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Phase 4: The 15% HP Threshold">
    Once the boss hits 15% HP, it will drop the **Astro Arm**. This is a critical moment. You must ensure you have a Sci-TV or Poly version unit ready to salvage this item to obtain the Astro Arm Tech Badge.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
Use the "Melancholy" music as a timer. The boss's attack patterns often sync with the intensity of the track. If the music reaches a crescendo, expect a Warp Attack or a Triple Laser blast shortly.
</Callout>

</Section>

**Trivia & Fun Facts:**
