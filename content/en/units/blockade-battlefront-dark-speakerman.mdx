<Section icon={Target} title="Skill Tree Path and Upgrade Order">

The skill tree is straightforward, but the order matters more than most players expect. Some upgrades unlock your real kit, while others simply make the character sturdier. If you rush durability too early, you delay the moves that actually define your pressure.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Start with Knife">
    Knife is automatically bought at **0 MiS**, so you begin with the base close-range kit already online.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Unlock Sound Improvement">
    Spend **300 MiS** to unlock the **F move**. This is one of the earliest quality-of-life upgrades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Add Multi-Task">
    Spend **200 MiS** to unlock the **H move**. This expands your combat options and helps the kit feel less linear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finish with Spear n' Knife">
    Spend **1200 MiS** to unlock the spear and knife move set, then return for durability upgrades.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Upgrade | Cost | Unlocks | Value |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Knife | **0 MiS** | Base kit | Mandatory starting point |
| Sound Improvement | **300 MiS** | **F move** | Early utility and smoother play |
| Multi-Task | **200 MiS** | **H move** | More flexibility in combat |
| Spear n' Knife | **1200 MiS** | Spear and knife set | Main kit expansion |
| Body Improvement | **500 MiS** | **-10% damage taken** | Better survivability |
| Body Improvement 2 | **1000 MiS** | **8 HP/s regeneration** | Stronger sustain between fights |

<Callout type="info" title="Upgrade Priority">
If you already have access to mastery progression, plan around the free **Sound Improvement** at mastery 25. That breakpoint changes the value of your early MiS spending.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Mastery Breakpoints and Combat Plan">

Mastery is where Dark Speakerman starts to feel much more complete. The breakpoints do not just raise numbers; they change how quickly you can access the kit and how cheaply you can keep improving it. That makes the 25, 50, and 80 milestones worth planning around instead of treating as passive bonuses.

| Fight Phase | Priority | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opening | Cast the aura before committing | Higher damage and better survivability |
| Engagement | Use leap slash or spear attack first | Secure contact without overextending |
| Burst | Spend red slashes, soundwave, or heavy stab | Convert the opening into real damage |
| Reset | Dash out or reposition with movement tools | Avoid getting stuck after cooldowns are spent |

| Common Mistake | Better Choice |
| --- | --- |
| Buying durability before core unlocks | Get the move unlocks first, then add defenses |
| Using spear throw as your opener every time | Save it for pressure, finishing, or cursor control |
| Spamming melee without the aura | Build a damage window before the full commit |
| Ignoring mastery 50 | Plan your upgrades around the lower-cost breakpoint |

<Callout type="success" title="Practical Loop">
Open with mobility, spend your burst inside the aura, and exit before the enemy resets. That loop fits the character’s rhythm better than extended brawls.
</Callout>
