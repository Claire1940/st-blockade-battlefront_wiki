<Section icon={Map} title="blockade battlefront speaker carrier Basics">

A speaker carrier build is not about stacking every loud unit you own. It is about building a lane that can survive early pressure, clear packed waves, and hold resources for the moment a Titan or late-game pick swings the run. In Blockade Battlefront, that usually means one main Speakermen-style damage piece, one control or support layer, and one emergency finisher.

The official game structure supports that idea well: the core loop is wave defense mixed with active movement, unit deployment, and Titan usage. If your team only hits hard on paper but cannot hold the base, the run collapses before the carry unit matters.

**Why the speaker lane works**
- Speakermen units fit crowded fights.
- Titans convert a stable defense into a wave break.
- Control pieces buy time for cooldowns and positioning.
- Balanced spending keeps the build alive past the opening waves.

| Core Element | Job | Carry Value | Best Use |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Speakermen | Sound pressure, AoE style damage, frontline support | High | Packed waves and sustained fights |
| TV Men | Control, repositioning, utility | Medium | Safer defense and enemy disruption |
| Cameramen | Balanced damage and tanking | Stable | Early progression and fallback lanes |
| Titans | Big swing damage and pressure reset | Very high | Bosses, hard waves, and late-game holds |

<Callout type="tip" title="Build Rule">
Do not let one flashy unit define the whole setup. A real carrier build needs at least one reliable damage lane, one survival layer, and one late-wave swing.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Build the Loadout">

The safest way to build this loadout is to start simple, then layer power only after your base stops leaking. That approach matches the game’s pace better than chasing a full damage fantasy on day one.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pick one main carry">
    Choose a Speakermen-side unit you can field consistently. Your main goal is not maximum burst; it is dependable wave control.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Add one control layer">
    Bring a utility unit or a safer frontline option so your speaker damage has time to work. A build that cannot buy time will lose value fast.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Learn the combat keys">
    The official controls include Q, E, R, T, Y, F, G, H, Z, X, C, V, B, plus double spacebar. Learn them before you push harder modes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Time Titans for spikes">
    Save Titan usage for dense enemy groups, boss windows, or a wave that would otherwise break the base.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Upgrade only what you use">
    Put Mastery and spending into the unit that appears in most runs. Random upgrades spread too thin and slow real progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The best mode for practicing this setup is not always the hardest one. Standard waves teach lane rhythm. Boss Rush teaches burst timing. Zombie Mode teaches how a speaker-heavy build handles enemy density. Astro-style content is useful if you want to test whether your team can adapt to special pressure patterns.

| Mode | What It Teaches | Speaker Carrier Value | Main Focus |

| Astro Invasion | Specialized enemy testing | Medium | Team flexibility |
